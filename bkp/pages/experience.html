Experience

Verizon

After my time in the Air Force, I joined Verizon as a network engineer. The pace was fast, but the goal was the same: to build and manage efficient communication networks.

At Verizon, I was more than a team member. I was responsible for designing systems from scratch. This involved creating a comprehensive system that met our clients' diverse needs, from power systems to data routing.

As a detail engineer and core equipment engineer, I was responsible for maintaining our systems. This involved understanding the entire network ecosystem, optimizing network performance, and addressing potential issues before they became problems.

But my role at Verizon wasn't just about technology. It was also about connecting with people: my colleagues, stakeholders, and clients. Each project was a chance to solve a problem and create a success story.

Throughout my time at Verizon, I was reminded of my mission from my Air Force days: to ensure clear, secure communication. At Verizon, this mission expanded to include speed, efficiency, and innovation.

It was a great experience.

Air Force

My journey in the Air Force started in Texas with Basic Military Training (BMT). From there, I moved to Biloxi, where I began to learn about networks and communications.

At Whiteman AFB, I balanced theoretical knowledge with hands-on experience. I was also given the opportunity to lead the Communications Focal Point.

In Afghanistan, I was responsible for maintaining secure communication channels, despite the challenging environment. On my way back, I stopped in Kyrgyzstan, where I had a chance to reflect and experience the local culture.

Back in Nevada, I continued to work on technical projects, delving into complex topics like cryptography and secure, private networks. In my free time, I expanded my personal hobbies and passions, exploring photography and video game development.

Throughout my time in the Air Force, I worked with companies like General Dynamics, Cisco, Juniper, and Ciena. I learned about all aspects of networking, from the physical layer to the application layer.

My mission was always clear: to ensure clear, secure communication. Looking back, I see that this journey was about more than just technology. It was about building relationships and making a difference.

It was a challenging but rewarding experience.

Education

I started my academic journey at New Mexico State University. However, I soon left to join the Air Force.

While in the Air Force, I continued my education at the Community College of the Air Force and the University of Phoenix online. The flexible curriculum allowed me to balance my military duties with my studies.

When the University of Phoenix was involved in a scandal, I transferred to Arizona State University with just 20 credits left to graduate.

At ASU, I focused on security, computer science, and information technology. I was able to apply the practical knowledge I gained in the Air Force to my academic studies.

Looking back, I see that each step of my academic journey, from New Mexico to Arizona, has contributed to my career in a unique way.

Education is a lifelong journey.
