inspirations

Richard Stallman ↗

Richard Stallman, a renowned programmer and activist, is an inspiration for his unwavering commitment to the principles of software freedom. As the founder of the Free Software Movement, Stallman has dedicated his life to promoting and defending freedom in computing. His creation of the GNU Operating System and the GNU General Public License has had a profound impact on the world of technology, promoting the values of freedom, cooperation, and community.

Linus Torvalds ↗

Linus Torvalds, the creator of Linux and Git, is a beacon of inspiration for his monumental contributions to open-source software. His creation of the Linux kernel, which forms the basis for the Linux operating system, has revolutionized the world of computing and has been instrumental in the rise of the open-source movement. His creation of Git, a distributed version control system, has transformed the way software is developed, enabling collaboration and innovation on a global scale.

Edward Snowden ↗

Edward Snowden, a former intelligence contractor and whistleblower, is an inspiration for his courage and commitment to transparency. His revelations about global surveillance programs have sparked a worldwide debate about privacy, government secrecy, and the balance between national security and information freedom. Snowden's actions have led to significant legal and policy changes and have had a lasting impact on the way we think about government surveillance and our rights to privacy in the digital age.

Julian Assange ↗

Julian Assange, the founder of WikiLeaks, is an inspiration for his dedication to transparency and his fearless pursuit of truth. His work with WikiLeaks has exposed corruption, war crimes, and abuses of power, sparking global debates and leading to significant political changes. Despite facing intense legal and political pressure, Assange has remained committed to his mission of creating a more transparent world.

Aaron Swartz ↗

Aaron Swartz, a programmer, writer, and activist, is an inspiration for his extraordinary contributions to the internet and his commitment to social justice. He co-authored the RSS 1.0 specification at age 14, co-founded Reddit, and led the fight against the Stop Online Piracy Act (SOPA). Swartz's work has had a profound impact on the internet and continues to inspire activists and technologists around the world.

Cory Doctorow ↗

Cory Doctorow is a science fiction author, activist, and journalist. He is a proponent of Creative Commons and has published all his books under its licenses, allowing and encouraging readers to copy and share his work. His writings, both fiction and non-fiction, explore themes of digital rights, privacy, and the impact of technology on society. His commitment to digital rights and his ability to use storytelling to explore complex technological issues is truly inspiring.

Eric S. Raymond ↗

Eric S. Raymond, often known as ESR, is an open-source advocate and developer, with a particular focus on software development and hacker culture. He has written a guide on how to ask questions in a way more likely to get a satisfactory answer, which is a resource I love and find incredibly useful. It's not just about getting answers, it's about the art of problem-solving and communication in the world of software development.
